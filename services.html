Distressed Asset Recovery Methodology

Our structured six-step approach ensures thorough investigation and maximum recovery for creditors and stakeholders.

1

Preliminary Assessment

Review financial records, corporate filings, and creditor information to establish the investigative foundation.

2

Corporate & Asset Intelligence

Analyse shareholding structures and related-party relationships to map the corporate landscape.

3

Financial Transaction Analysis

Identify irregular transfers, suspicious patterns, or asset dissipation through detailed transactional review.

4

Asset Tracing

Trace properties, vehicles, business interests, and other holdings linked to the distressed entity or its directors.

5

Investigation Reporting

Deliver structured reports for insolvency practitioners, creditors, and legal advisors with actionable findings.

6

Litigation Support

Provide evidence and analytical support for recovery proceedings, court applications, and compliance matters.

Government Asset Control & Recovery Framework

Maiwashe Forensics supports government departments across the complete asset control lifecycle — from procurement through to forensic investigation and recovery of missing or irregularly disposed assets.

Procurement

Acquisition approval, vendor documentation, and regulatory compliance

→

Asset Register

Recording, database organisation, and custodian assignment

→

Tagging

Barcode/RFID identification and unique numbering

Verification

Physical site checks and asset register reconciliation

→

Investigation

Forensic tracing of missing or irregularly disposed assets

→

Recovery

Asset retrieval, legal action, and compliance documentation

Compliance Framework: Maiwashe Forensics operates within the Public Finance Management Act (PFMA), Municipal Finance Management Act (MFMA), and the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA).
